Štai Mano Evangelija:

To quote the poet, "Susapnavu dimną sapną.” The past has been archived; the future is algorithmically determined. Time and space have melded into one as binary materiality is dissolved into binary signals. Our holy mašinas have been updated, upgraded, and uplifted to the divine. Can the digital medium really become a digital medium? Our new ghosts have stumbled upon eternity and killed the Nepakartojama in the process. Our sensors can only observe as our hands act on autopilot. The world decays as we drape the skin of God upon our organless bodies. It fits too tight over where our stomachs used to be.

The art:

    1. Graham Hardwood & Matsuko Yokokoji, 2005. LUNGS
    2. John Russel, 2015. SQRRL
    3. Kai Altmann, 2008. Dreamcaptchas

Each piece details a melding of the material with the immaterial. Each piece details Freud’s Prosthetic God. A mastery of the immaterial, becomes a loss of the immateriality of the material. LUNGS details the breaths of the oppressed through digital archives, SQRRL covers the eviction of the feral to make room for the “enlightened”. Oppression, time, and digital transcendence all blend into one–the death of the Nepakartojama. Dreamcaptchas shows the ugly skin that covers the beautiful tomorrow. Becoming God is a godless pursuit.
